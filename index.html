Before You Book

What should I bring to my appointment?

Your government ID, cash or a card for the deposit, and any reference images you've collected. Eat a full meal beforehand — tattooing on an empty stomach is rough. Wear or bring clothing that gives your artist easy access to the placement area.

How much will my tattoo cost?

Our shop minimum is $80. Hourly rates run $150–$200 depending on artist and complexity — Sasha's portrait work carries a premium rate given the demand. We quote every custom piece honestly after consultation. No surprises on session day.

What's the deposit policy?

A deposit is required to hold your booking slot and covers your artist's design time. It comes off the final price of your tattoo. Deposits are non-refundable if you no-show or cancel within 48 hours of your appointment. Rescheduling with proper notice keeps your deposit on file.

Do you do touch-ups?

Yes. If your tattoo heals with spots or inconsistencies that resulted from our work, we'll touch it up at no charge — typically scheduled 8–12 weeks after the original session once healing is complete. Touch-ups caused by poor aftercare are charged at a reduced rate.

Can I see my design before the appointment?

Yes — your artist will share the design with you before session day. Revisions are part of the process. We ask that feedback comes in one consolidated round so your artist can work without back-and-forth edits that dilute the design direction.

Aftercare Guide

The First 24 Hours

Keep the wrap on for 2–4 hours or as long as your artist recommends. When you remove it, gently wash with unscented antibacterial soap and lukewarm water using only your fingertips — no washcloths. Pat dry with a clean paper towel. Apply a thin layer of unscented lotion or the aftercare product your artist provided. Do not re-wrap.

Washing & Moisturizing

Wash 2–3 times a day for the first week. Use unscented soap only — fragrance irritates fresh ink. After washing, apply a thin coat of unscented moisturizer. Thin layers. You want the skin to breathe, not suffocate under a thick coat of lotion.

What to Avoid

No soaking — pools, ocean, baths, hot tubs — until fully healed (minimum 3 weeks). No direct sun on fresh ink. No picking, scratching, or peeling the flaky skin. No tight clothing rubbing the area. These aren't suggestions — they're the difference between a great heal and a patchy one.

Sun Protection & Long-term Care

UV is the number one killer of tattoo vibrancy. Once healed, apply SPF 50+ sunscreen to your tattoo every time it will be exposed to sun. This applies forever, not just the first summer. Color work and fine-line details degrade fastest under UV exposure.
