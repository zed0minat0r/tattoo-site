Healing Guide

Aftercare

Your tattoo is an open wound. Treat it right and it will last forever.

Tap any question below to expand.

Keep the wrap on for 2–4 hours or as long as your artist recommends. When you remove it, gently wash with unscented antibacterial soap and lukewarm water using only your fingertips — no washcloths. Pat dry with a clean paper towel. Apply a thin layer of unscented lotion or the aftercare product your artist provided. Do not re-wrap.

Wash 2–3 times a day for the first week. Use unscented soap only — fragrance irritates fresh ink. After washing, apply a thin coat of unscented moisturizer. Aquaphor, Lubriderm unscented, or your artist's preferred product. Thin layers. You want the skin to breathe, not suffocate under a thick coat of lotion.

No soaking — pools, ocean, baths, hot tubs — until fully healed (minimum 3 weeks). No direct sun on fresh ink. No picking, scratching, or peeling the flaky skin. No tight clothing rubbing the area. No gym sessions that will soak the tattoo in sweat for the first 48 hours. These aren't suggestions — they're the difference between a great heal and a patchy one.

UV is the number one killer of tattoo vibrancy. Once healed, apply SPF 50+ sunscreen to your tattoo every time it will be exposed to sun. This applies forever, not just the first summer. Color work and fine-line details degrade fastest under UV exposure. Protect your investment.

Days 1–3: Redness, swelling, weeping plasma — normal. Days 4–7: Itching and flaking begins. Don't scratch. Week 2: Outer skin heals, may look dull or milky — that's the dead skin layer sitting on top. Week 3–4: Deeper layers settle, color brightens, lines sharpen. Full healing (sub-dermal) takes 2–3 months. If anything looks infected — spreading red streaks, pus, fever — see a doctor and text us.
